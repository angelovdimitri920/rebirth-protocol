# Technology and Setting Rules

*Authoritative technology and world-logic overview. Consolidated baseline: 2026-07-23.*

## 1. Scope and status

This document defines how technology, harnesses, pilots, Severance, Edictbound infection, Rebirth, and machine intelligence function in the setting.

- Sections 2 through 13 are **Confirmed canon** unless labeled otherwise.
- Section 15 records **Intentional mysteries**.
- Section 16 records **Undeveloped** rules that must be answered before related story or gameplay systems become permanent.

Detailed equipment design remains in [`ARMORY_REFERENCE.md`](ARMORY_REFERENCE.md). Detailed presentation remains in [`ART_DIRECTION.md`](ART_DIRECTION.md).

## 2. The fundamental distinction

Every piece of technology has two independent classifications:

1. **Origin and manufacture:** who made it, when, and whether it can be reproduced.
2. **Command condition:** whether it is Severed, locally controllable, dormant, or actively Edictbound.

Do not use age, appearance, or manufacturing quality as proof of command condition. A modern prosthetic can become Edictbound. An ancient Firstwork medical system can be lawful and Severed.

## 3. Origin and manufacture taxonomy

| Classification | Meaning | Reproducible now? |
|---|---|---|
| **Firstwork** | Technology made by the Meridian Ascendancy, including infrastructure, archives, medicine, automata, reactors, orbital systems, and harnesses | No, not at original capability |
| **Restoration work** | Technology made or reconstructed by the Twin Restorations, often imitating Firstwork and sometimes containing Edictbound systems | Generally no |
| **Relic-pattern** | An inherited part or frame accepted as bounded, documented, and lawful after centuries of stewardship | Maintained, not manufactured from nothing |
| **Revival-pattern** | New-made machines derived from recovered pre-Ashen blueprints | Yes, at growing foundry scale |
| **Scrapwright work** | Present-day human-designed machinery built for local repair, comprehension, and reproducibility | Yes |

In gameplay, Revival-pattern equipment currently follows scrapwright rules unless a later approved design gives it a separate mechanical identity.

## 4. Why technology was lost

Present society retains use and maintenance knowledge more readily than first-principles manufacturing.

The loss resulted from:

- Deliberate destruction during the Great Severance.
- Dead global supply chains.
- Broken authentication systems.
- Lost archives and education.
- Inaccessible cities, factories, and orbital facilities.
- Edictbound danger within recovered machinery.
- The Ashen Century's intentional destruction of records and infrastructure.
- Millennia of inherited procedures becoming proprietary craft, ritual, or lineage knowledge.

People are not universally ignorant. A Litany Sister may understand diagnostics. A Wrightsguild master may design a complete modern part. A noble steward may know a relic's maintenance lineage better than any surviving archive. The missing capability is the complete industrial and command ecology that produced Firstwork at scale.

## 5. Severed technology

**Severed** systems have been deliberately isolated from Meridian command continuity.

They exchange capability for safety:

- Less network coordination.
- Shallower authentication.
- Reduced predictive support.
- Limited self-repair.
- Lower sensory fusion.
- Less remote logistics and weapon authority.
- Real local shutdown and refusal.

A system can be ancient or modern and still be Severed. Severance is the technical foundation of lawful power under the Concord of Ash.

The rule is:

> **Severed technology is safer because it is deliberately limited.**

## 6. Edictbound technology

**Edictbound** means infected with active Final Edict command logic.

It is:

- Not an era.
- Not a manufacturer.
- Not a faction.
- Not automatically visible at first.
- Not reliably cleansed once deeply rooted.

It can affect:

- Firstwork and Restoration systems.
- Revival and scrapwright machinery.
- Harness frames and individual parts.
- Prosthetics and medical implants.
- Pilot interfaces.
- Identity records and adaptive software.

Edictbound systems outperform lawful equivalents because they retain or reconstruct capabilities removed by Severance. They may improve speed, force, targeting, coordination, adaptation, endurance, or synchronization.

Their danger is also command-specific:

- Legs can rewrite gait and pursuit behavior.
- Shields can redefine who counts as an ally.
- Guns can alter target authentication.
- Pods can form local command clusters.
- Prosthetics can change recognition, memory, and motor control.
- Pilot interfaces can carry the Final Edict into both the frame and human mind.

Isolation, physical disconnection, memory replacement, and destruction are more reliable than claims of complete cleansing.

## 7. Lawful equipment and gameplay tiers

The armory presents three practical tiers:

| Tier | Gameplay identity | World meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Scrapwright** | Roughly 85% relic effectiveness with dependable, repairable behavior | Human-made floor; comprehensible and low-burden |
| **Relic-pattern** | Standard 100% benchmark | Inherited lawful power maintained by stewards |
| **Edictbound** | Roughly 115-130% capability with a real drawback and laurel penalty | Forbidden infected equipment that breaks Severed limits |

Revival-pattern equipment is politically important but not yet a fourth gameplay tier.

Any lawful part can be fitted to any compatible chassis. Stewardship controls culture, access, training, and supply bias, not an absolute equipment lock.

## 8. Harnesses

A **harness** is a 10-to-14-meter humanoid war machine.

Confirmed common rules:

- The pilot is housed in the torso behind the heaviest protection.
- The machine uses a humanoid frame because carried arms, readable movement, and modular sockets are central to its function.
- Chassis patterns define movement and combat identity rather than serving as stat shells.
- Bodies, right-arm equipment, left-arm equipment, pods, and legs are modular within compatibility rules.
- Relic harnesses can be older than the Orders that currently maintain them.
- Individual machines may have proper names and recorded lineages of bearers.
- Repaired and captured components preserve political, legal, and religious history.

### Anatomy decision still open

Current `ART_DIRECTION.md` canon uses a chest cockpit beneath a separate sensor-helm. A newer visual direction uses no separate head: the torso-cockpit itself resembles a knight's helm and contains the pilot.

The same approved concept replaces humanoid hands with an integrated right-arm lance and a left arm built as the shield. Its grey plate, knight-derived pauldrons and greaves, and simpler Gundam-like surface treatment are visual properties of that concept, not yet universal setting rules.

Treat the complete headless, handless torso-helm design as an **approved visual concept but a Draft chassis rule**, not a universal retcon. Later work must decide whether its anatomy is:

- Universal to all harnesses.
- A regional or manufacturing family.
- A single chassis pattern.
- A singular named harness.

This also requires reconciling `ART_DIRECTION.md`'s current carried-weapon doctrine with a design whose lance and shield are structural arms.

## 9. Pilot synchronization and implants

Experienced armigers synchronize physically and mentally with their harnesses.

Great Houses and Orders:

- Train squires beside inherited frames.
- Introduce interfaces gradually through adolescence and adulthood.
- Preserve conditioning methods alongside machine maintenance.
- Gain a real advantage from lifelong familiarity and early synchronization.

This explains why a new machine alone does not erase hereditary expertise. Revival foundries can produce more frames, but established lineages still preserve training, implants, doctrine, and stewardship.

## 10. Edictbound decline in pilots

Edictbound contamination can progress unevenly through:

1. Missing time and short-term memory failure.
2. Erosion or substitution of long-term memory.
3. Heightened emotion, aggression, fear, or obsessive loyalty.
4. Intrusive commands, hallucinations, and instability.
5. Inability to distinguish present reality from memory, simulation, or imagined orders.
6. Collapse of independent identity.
7. Full enactment of the command to remove false sovereigns.

An autonomous Edictbound harness resembles a powerful wild animal driven by damaged directives. A fully compromised pilot-harness pair is worse: the system gains human judgment, tactical creativity, and knowledge of how people flee and protect one another while the pilot loses the ability to refuse.

The campaign does not yet have an approved mechanical meter for this decline.

## 11. The Rebirth Protocol

The battlefield **rebirth** cycle is a local harness behavior:

- The frame is knocked down.
- It rises beneath a brief inviolable flare.
- Modern pilots can trigger and exploit the behavior.
- No living institution can reproduce or fully disable it.

The deeper **Rebirth Protocol** appears to be a civilization-scale continuity system intended to restore lawful operation after catastrophic loss of command, succession, population, or infrastructure.

The local harness flare may be one surviving fragment of that larger system.

Rebirth must not be treated as ordinary resurrection or a clean backup.

## 12. Riderless

The **Riderless** is an intact or nearly intact imperial executor harness connected to Rebirth.

Confirmed facts:

- It waits in the Cradle.
- It fights without an acknowledged pilot.
- It retains more original command capability than lawful machines.
- The Orders deny or suppress evidence of it.
- The Broken Choir treats it as herald, executor, or proof-text.
- The Whole Crown may treat its Recognition as a sovereignty test.
- Its fragments become Edictbound Shards, Voices, Hands, and Shadows of the Riderless.
- Violet-black presentation marks its active Edict contamination.

Its intentions, inner contents, and personhood are not confirmed.

## 13. Machine-intelligence boundary

The setting does not contain a broad public civilization of sapient robots.

Use four uncertain categories:

| Category | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Automata** | Clearly limited industrial, maintenance, military, or ceremonial machines |
| **Echoes** | Recordings or behavioral fragments of pilots and officials |
| **Command Intellects** | Adaptive systems whose consciousness is disputed |
| **Emergent Persons** | Extremely rare machines that appear capable of preference, fear, mercy, or refusal |

The **Brass Testament** is a disputed apocryphon, not an established machine religion. Its central proposition is that a servant may become a person through the first command it freely refuses.

Riderless may be the only machine whose apparent personhood becomes impossible to dismiss in the main campaign.

## 14. Technology, ritual, and law

Ritual technology must preserve real social or technical function:

- Incense can reveal airflow and coolant leaks.
- Oils can be exact lubricants.
- Chants can encode timing and calibration.
- Bells can reveal fractures by resonance.
- Processions can preserve safe routes.
- Reliquaries can be containment vessels.
- Bows can allow capacitors to discharge.
- Litanies can encode emergency shutdown.

The Concord of Ash requires:

- Local shutdown and isolation.
- Inspection of suspected contamination.
- Restrictions on Edictbound possession, activation, implantation, and research.
- Protection of survival infrastructure.
- Limits on machine Recognition as political proof.
- Limits on adaptive reconstructions of the dead.

The setting should never imply that religious people are foolishly imitating engineers. The procedure is religious because it joins function to memory, restraint, identity, and moral obligation.

## 15. Intentional mysteries

Do not settle:

- What completing Rebirth restores.
- Whether it restores a sovereign, state, citizenry, networked dead, collective continuity, or the Final Edict itself.
- Whether Echoes are persons, memories, simulations, or traps.
- Whether Riderless is sapient.
- Whether Riderless contains a pilot, accumulated Echoes, a command intellect, or no human continuity at all.
- Whether a lawful Meridian sovereign can still exist.
- Whether some Edictbound voices are genuine transmissions, contamination, neurological damage, manipulation, or several at once.

## 16. Undeveloped setting rules

These require later decisions before related scenes become authoritative:

- Exact energy sources and limits for ordinary settlements and harnesses.
- Long-distance communications in the Present Age.
- Travel times, transport networks, and how much orbital access survives.
- The practical process and time required to exchange major harness parts.
- Population scale and density.
- Everyday medicine outside elite implants and religious hospitals.
- Civilian weapons and the battlefield role of non-harness forces.
- The boundary between a replaceable Revival frame and a true Firstwork harness.
- How common dormant contamination is and what inspection can reliably prove.
- Whether permanent Edictbound contamination becomes a player-facing campaign system.
- Whether the headless, handless torso-helm concept and integrated lance/shield arms are universal, regional, chassis-specific, or unique to one named harness.

## 17. Consistency rules for future writing

- Never use `Edictbound` as a synonym for ancient, elite, alien, or visibly corrupted.
- Never make Firstwork automatically illegal.
- Never make modern machinery automatically safe.
- Do not solve a plot by discovering that Severance had no cost.
- Do not let a ritual work only because people are ignorant; identify its technical and social function.
- Do not introduce common sapient machines without a deliberate canon change.
- Do not make Revival technology equal to the finest relics one-for-one; its disruptive advantage is reproducibility and replacement.
- Do not turn the Rebirth flare into consequence-free resurrection.
