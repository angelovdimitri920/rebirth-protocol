# Present-Day World and Atlas

*Authoritative present-world and geography framework. Consolidated baseline: 2026-07-23.*

## 1. Scope and status

This document owns the world as it exists around **3800 AFE**:

- Political geography.
- Countries, cities, territories, and borders.
- Climate, ecology, travel, trade, and communications.
- Lasthold and other named locations.
- The physical placement of Houses, Orders, faiths, and factions.

The present political pattern and named locations in Sections 2 through 6 are **Confirmed canon**. Most map-scale detail is **Undeveloped**. This is currently the largest worldbuilding gap in the project.

Do not infer countries, continents, borders, languages, or distances from an Order's historical inspiration.

## 2. Present-age world pattern

The Present Age has lasted roughly eight centuries. The world contains functioning states and regional systems, but no universal political authority.

The political map includes:

- Large kingdoms and successor blocs.
- Duchies, baronies, principalities, and marches.
- Chartered cities, free ports, and trade leagues.
- Great House domains.
- Knightly commanderies and independent Orders.
- Guild territories and foundry concessions.
- Temple lands and religious jurisdictions.
- Mercenary companies and Hedge Errantry strongholds.
- Mobile confederations and frontier communities.

Large powers dominate their regions but depend on smaller states, cities, Houses, Orders, guilds, and religious institutions. No power can conquer widely without producing coalitions, internal revolt, overextension, or fear of Edictbound escalation.

This creates a durable but unstable balance:

- Borders move.
- Small states are annexed.
- Large states fracture through succession crises or civil war.
- Trade and religious jurisdictions cross political borders.
- Harness ownership can matter more than nominal territory.
- A city controlling a foundry, reactor, seed vault, or ancient road can bargain above its size.

The world is neo-feudal because authority is layered and negotiated, not because every region resembles one historical form of medieval Europe.

## 3. Immediate political fault line

The **War of the Open Forges**, twenty to twenty-five years before the story, changed the military balance.

A coalition of smaller duchies, baronies, ports, and trade cities used:

- Trade wealth.
- Independent guilds.
- Mercenary organization.
- Reproducible Revival-pattern harnesses.
- Replaceable parts and trained pilots.

They defeated a larger, older, poorly governed kingdom and its hereditary military order.

The war created the present crisis:

- Great Houses fear the loss of their inherited monopoly.
- Smaller states seek foundry access before their rivals.
- Guilds want political rights equal to their new military importance.
- Mercenaries can now field credible harness formations.
- Religious institutions disagree over mass rearmament.
- The Broken Choir sees an expanding population of compatible frames.
- The Ash Levelers see both liberation from hereditary rule and the threat of industrialized harness war.

The exact belligerents, borders, decisive battles, and settlement remain **Undeveloped**.

## 6. Institutions that shape geography

The modern map cannot be designed only as colored countries. It must also show overlapping networks:

### Survival infrastructure

- Second Sun thermal sanctuaries.
- Hospitaller hospitals and refugee roads.
- Seed vaults, granaries, water works, and heating systems.
- Protected sites under the Concord of Ash.

### Movement and trade

- Wrightsguild foundries and repair houses.
- Chartered ports and trade leagues.
- Pilgrimage routes.
- Mercenary roads and Passage circuits.
- Ruin routes controlled by Orders or Lantern Pilgrims.

### Religious jurisdiction

- Communion dioceses and the Empty See.
- Banner Court lineage territories and Great Musters.
- Second Sun sanctuary networks.
- Whole Crown courts and ministry enclaves.
- Local shrines recognized differently by several traditions.

### Military authority

- Great House domains.
- Order commanderies.
- City Warden jurisdictions.
- Foundry alliances.
- Mercenary and Hedge Errantry territories.

A settlement may owe taxes to a prince, religious law to the Communion, heating access to a Thermarch, harness protection to an Order, and repair dependence to the Wrightsguild.

## 7. Environmental inheritance

The world still carries physical consequences of its history:

- Meridian ruins and inaccessible cities.
- Dormant or active orbital infrastructure.
- Edictbound patrol zones and sealed arsenals.
- Regional cold caused by failed or weaponized climate systems.
- Poisoned agricultural ground.
- Ruined reactors and frozen cities.
- Recovered roads and machines still following ancient routes.
- Landscapes transformed into consecrated Lists.

The world should not appear uniformly ruined. Some regions have rebuilt for millennia. Contrast is essential:

- Stable old cities beside forbidden ruins.
- Productive farmland beside poisoned belts.
- Working foundries beside relics no one can reproduce.
- Safe pilgrimage roads crossing machine-haunted territory.
- Wealthy Order districts near patchwork mercenary quarters.

## 8. Everyday economy and class

Confirmed economic pressures:

- Relic stewardship produces inherited military privilege.
- Foundries and Revival manufacture create new urban and guild power.
- Scrapwright work is the repairable foundation of ordinary machinery.
- Heat, water, seed, transport, and repair access create political authority.
- Harness war consumes resources that could sustain civilian populations.
- Captured equipment carries financial, legal, religious, and military value.
- Guilds can make complete modern parts but remain excluded from ownership of many ancestral relics.

The exact coinage, taxation systems, labor law, serfdom patterns, market scale, and international trade goods are **Undeveloped**.

## 9. Lasthold development requirements

Lasthold is the first location that needs full development because it anchors the player's daily experience.

Confirmed functions:

- Herald's hall and event board.
- Hangar and customization.
- Test yard or Squires' Yard access.
- Wrightsguild repair and scrapwright stock.
- Stewardship shops associated with major equipment traditions.
- Named armigers who remain present between events.
- Entry into the Grand Passage.

Required future decisions:

| Category | Needed answer |
|---|---|
| Government | Who rules Lasthold, and why can rival Orders operate there? |
| Neutrality | What law prevents a faction from seizing the Grand Passage? |
| Districts | Where do the Herald, guilds, Orders, common residents, pilgrims, and foreign delegations live? |
| Economy | What supports the city outside tournament traffic? |
| Infrastructure | How are heat, water, food, power, transit, and repairs maintained? |
| History | Why was this location chosen, and what survived beneath it? |
| Social hierarchy | What separates armigers, squires, guild workers, commoners, refugees, and pilgrims? |
| Threats | Which factions infiltrate, pressure, or attack the city? |
| Visual identity | Which architecture belongs to the city rather than visiting factions? |

## 10. Atlas development template

Every country or major territory should eventually define:

1. Name and endonym.
2. Location and neighboring regions.
3. Climate, terrain, and ecological scars.
4. Population centers and rural structure.
5. Government and succession.
6. Relationship among ruler, Houses, Orders, guilds, and faiths.
7. Survival infrastructure.
8. Economy, trade, and foundry access.
9. Harness strength and military doctrine.
10. Position on Revival manufacture.
11. Position on the Concord of Ash.
12. Public and secret relationship to Edictbound technology.
13. Languages, customs, clothing, and architecture.
14. Important ruins, routes, Lists, and pilgrimage sites.
15. Current conflicts and campaign relevance.

No country should be built only around an aesthetic. Its institutions, material base, and strategic pressures must explain its culture.

## 12. Present-world consistency rules

- Do not make the world one homogeneous wasteland.
- Do not treat all political authority as territorial state power.
- Do not assign a real historical culture directly to an Order's territory.
- Do not place every major institution in Lasthold.
- Do not make Revival manufacture common enough to erase relic prestige overnight.
- Do not make relic scarcity so absolute that cities, guilds, and modern industry cease to function.
- Do not create borders before establishing trade, infrastructure, military pressure, and religious jurisdiction.
- Do not use the Grand Passage as a substitute for all war. It limits conflict; it does not end politics or violence.
