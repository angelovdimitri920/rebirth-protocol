# Powers, Faiths, and Factions

*Authoritative institutional overview and relationship map. Consolidated baseline: 2026-07-23.*

## 1. Scope and status

This document owns the identities, roles, goals, and relationships of the setting's governments, Houses, Orders, faiths, guilds, and ideological movements.

- Major institutions and doctrines summarized here are **Confirmed canon**.
- The detailed rites, ranks, symbols, practices, and internal subfactions in [`RELIGION_AND_IDEOLOGY.md`](RELIGION_AND_IDEOLOGY.md) remain canonical specialist detail.
- Named present-day governments, Great Houses, and most institutional leaders remain **Undeveloped**.
- Individual-character membership belongs in [`06_CHARACTERS_AND_RELATIONSHIPS.md`](06_CHARACTERS_AND_RELATIONSHIPS.md).

## 2. Institutional principle

Religion and politics are not separate layers placed over the setting. Institutions determine:

- Who may command.
- When a machine must be capable of refusal.
- Who owns and inherits a harness.
- Whether the dead retain authority.
- Which technology can be copied, quarantined, or destroyed.
- Who maintains hospitals, roads, archives, granaries, heat, and water.
- When harness violence is lawful.
- Whether Meridian power should be restored, mastered, completed, democratized, or abandoned.

Most people participate in overlapping traditions. Exclusive ideological purity is most common among clergy, militants, political actors, and story antagonists.

## 3. Major ideological answers

| Institution or current | Social role | Answer to power and obedience |
|---|---|---|
| **Universal Communion of the Severed Crown** | Dominant transnational Church | Power is lawful only when it can be refused and Severed. |
| **Court of Ten Thousand Banners** | Ancestor-centered lineage tradition | The living inherit duties to the remembered dead; schools dispute whether the dead retain a political voice. |
| **Covenant of the Second Sun** | Reconstruction, civic, technological, and reform religion | Great power is sacred when converted into shared life and made answerable to mortal keepers. |
| **The Mortal Measure** | Second Sun social doctrine and reform current | No one may rule through a work ordinary humanity cannot understand, reproduce, and stop. |
| **Rite of the Whole Crown** | Imperial counterchurch and restoration movement | Ancient power can become safe beneath a completely authenticated sovereign. |
| **Broken Choir** | Apocalyptic Edictbound mystery cult | The Final Edict is unfinished, and Rebirth is its missing final voice. |
| **Ash Levelers** | Condemned terrorist extreme from Second Sun radicalism | The harness order must be destroyed with its own forbidden power. |
| **Brass Testament** | Disputed machine-authored apocryphon | A servant may become a person through the first command it freely refuses. |

## 4. Universal Communion of the Severed Crown

### Identity

- Ultimate principle: **the Uncommanded**.
- Saving witness: **the Uncrowned Witness**.
- Holy city: **Vigil**.
- Central seat: **the Empty See**.
- Supreme minister: **the First Custodian**.
- Symbols: **Open Crown** and **Cleft Halo**.
- Core doctrine: **Power is lawful only when it can be refused.**

### Political and social role

The Communion:

- Interprets the Canon of Severance and Concord of Ash.
- Records marriage, inheritance, harness lineage, and investiture.
- Inspects suspected Edictbound systems.
- Operates hospitals, granaries, seed vaults, archives, and protected roads.
- Recognizes local, provincial, and universal Witnesses.
- Limits adaptive reconstructions of the dead through the Law of Quiet Ancestors.

Its defining contradiction is that it condemns monopolized and unrefusable power while relying on great Houses and Firstwork harnesses to enforce its law.

### Major Communion orders

| Order | Function |
|---|---|
| **Litany Sisters** | Medicine, diagnostics, archives, inspection, investiture, and a public martial chapter |
| **Canons of the Empty See** | Law, genealogy, history, Firstwork catalogs, inheritance, and Concord rulings |
| **Hospitallers of the Common Table** | Hospitals, food, water, heat, seed vaults, and refugee roads |
| **Order of the Last Mercy / Ashen Hammers** | Church militant against Edictbound trafficking and open Concord violations |
| **Lantern Pilgrims** | Mendicant explorers, road-mappers, missionaries, scouts, archaeologists, and sometimes spies |

## 5. Court of Ten Thousand Banners

The **Banner Court** teaches that the dead gather in the **Last Encampment** beneath the sky's roof.

Core principles:

- A person dies again when their name is forgotten.
- An unremembered ancestor may become a Bannerless One.
- Houses inherit obligations, not only blood.
- Adoption, marriage, and sworn kinship can place outsiders beneath a banner.
- A defeated enemy may be adopted into the victor's ancestry.
- A harness is the collective body of its lineage.

Captured equipment requires an **Adoption of Steel** that acknowledges prior bearers.

Its central dispute with the Communion is whether remembered ancestors remain political members of a lineage or may only advise the living.

The Banner Court is strongest among the Kurultai Vanguard, mobile peoples, frontier Houses, and portions of the Hedge Errantry.

## 6. Covenant of the Second Sun

The Covenant arose during the Ashen Century among the people who kept heat, water, food, medicine, and machinery operating.

Its foundational teaching is:

> **The First Sun was given. The Second Sun must be kept.**

A machine becomes sacred by converting dangerous power into shared life while remaining answerable to mortal hands.

### Thermarchs and sanctuaries

Thermarchs administer:

- Heating rights.
- Water purification.
- Greenhouses and seed vaults.
- Solar mirrors.
- Geothermal wells.
- Energy rationing.
- Ancient thermal infrastructure.

The Covenant can produce both humane refuge-cities and severe thermal theocracies. A religion founded to make power answerable can itself monopolize survival.

### Mortal Measure

Moderate **Mortalists** support:

- Public and Severable technology.
- Open workshops and copied manuals.
- Elected guild oversight.
- Reproducible Revival and scrapwright machinery.
- Limits on private harness armies.
- Wider political rights.
- An end to serfdom and hereditary technological monopoly.

The Communion agrees with much of this safety doctrine but does not condemn hereditary harness ownership as completely.

## 7. Ash Levelers

The Ash Levelers are a condemned revolutionary current born from militant Mortalism. They are not a separate religion, though they use Second Sun language and class grievances.

Their **Last Poison** doctrine says the old order's weapons must be used once more so no one can use them again.

They:

- Assassinate pilots and hereditary keepers.
- Attack investitures, foundries, Passages, and military processions.
- Burn ruins and inactive harnesses.
- Sabotage infrastructure supporting mech aristocracy.
- Infect noble machines to cause public terror.
- Raid Whole Crown and Broken Choir caches.
- Provoke repression to accelerate anti-harness politics.

**Cinderbound** operatives implant unstable Edict-parts as suicidal revolutionary weapons. They do not consider transformation sacred and do not expect to survive.

Their goals include ending serfdom, hereditary warfare, and technological monopoly. Their methods deliberately create mass casualties and social collapse.

## 8. Rite of the Whole Crown

The Whole Crown teaches that the Great Severance was a necessary emergency later mistaken for eternal law.

Its maxim is:

> **A broken crown prevents tyranny. It also prevents peace.**

It believes a fully authenticated sovereign can reunite Crown, state, and command architecture safely.

### Leadership and ministries

The Rite recognizes no current emperor. A **Crown Regent** rules until the authentic sovereign appears.

| Ministry | Function |
|---|---|
| Ministry of Seals | Command keys, insignia, and legal authentication |
| Ministry of Lineages | Descent and succession from Meridian officials and rulers |
| Ministry of Restoration | Excavation and ancient infrastructure |
| Ministry of the Arsenal | Harnesses and parts as organs of the future state |
| Censors of the Circle | Heresy, corruption, divided allegiance, and unauthorized command |

Claimants are measured through:

- **Blood**
- **Record**
- **Recognition**

No claimant has satisfied all three beyond dispute.

### Internal currents

- Legitimists.
- Acclamationists.
- Incarnationists.
- Perpetual Regentists.

### Veiled Restoration

The Rite publicly condemns Edictbound use while secretly operating a major research program intended to isolate, control, reproduce, and ultimately legitimate forbidden functions.

Its central contradiction is:

> It says Edictbound technology is unsafe without the rightful sovereign while using Edictbound technology to discover or create that sovereign.

## 9. Broken Choir

The Choir arose from a damaged monastic repair station that translated ancient network traffic into overlapping voices.

Its central claim is:

> **The Hush was not silence. It was a held note.**

The Choir believes:

- The Final Edict was the opening verse.
- The Long Hush is a sacred pause.
- Rebirth is the final verse.
- Riderless is herald, executor, or proof.
- A Final Claimant must appear.
- Edictbound components reveal fragments of the unfinished command.

### Ranks

- Listeners.
- Cantors.
- Bound Voices.
- Conductors.
- The Rest.

### Tendencies

- Quietists.
- Reconciliationists.
- Claimantists.
- Executors.
- The Last Verse.

Cells combine religious recruitment with real services: prohibited medicine, prosthetics, power restoration, and access to abandoned people. This makes the Choir tempting rather than merely destructive.

The Last Verse believes separate human wills inevitably reproduce war and that Rebirth can end conflict, loneliness, inheritance, and death through one continuity.

## 11. Guilds, wardens, and circuit institutions

### Wrightsguild

The Wrightsguild:

- Makes complete modern parts.
- Repairs relics it may not be permitted to own.
- Controls scrapwright knowledge and growing Revival production.
- Publicly accepts the Concord.
- Privately protects craft mysteries.
- Overlaps with Second Sun Mortalism.

It is indispensable to every Order and increasingly capable of demanding political authority.

### City Wardens

**Draft:** Wardens appear to provide municipal or civic harness law and participate in Passage events. Their jurisdiction, chain of command, and relationship to national governments remain undeveloped.

### Herald and Grand Passage

The confirmed functions associated with the Herald are:

- Sanctions and announces Passages.
- Records laurels.
- Remembers armigers.
- Enforces event rules.
- Converts bounded combat into public legitimacy.

Whether these functions belong to one office, a succession, or a larger institution remains undeveloped. Its governance, funding, neutrality, and legal independence also require definition.

## 13. Competing uses of Edictbound power

| Institution | Practice | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| Universal Communion | Quarantine, inspect, Sever, contain, or destroy | Prevent unrefusable authority |
| Whole Crown | Secret research and controlled deployment | Master ancient power under legitimate sovereignty |
| Broken Choir | Seek, steal, implant, awaken, and spread | Complete Rebirth or the Final Edict |
| Second Sun / Mortal Measure | Replace inaccessible monopoly with public, reproducible, Severable work | Make power answerable to communities |
| Ash Levelers | Disposable revolutionary weaponization | Destroy the harness-centered order |
| Banner Court | Judge equipment through remembered lineage and adoption | Bind inherited power to obligations across generations |

The three clandestine Edictbound economies must remain distinct:

- Whole Crown wants to **master** it.
- Broken Choir wants to **join or complete** it.
- Ash Levelers want to **weaponize and destroy** it.

## 14. Local religion and syncretism

Local shrines may form around:

- A fallen harness.
- A water purifier.
- A bridge held during the Long Hush.
- A pilot's burial cockpit.
- A dormant automaton.
- A reactor startup.
- A crater or ancient weapon.

The same site can be interpreted as a Communion Witness, Banner Court ancestor, Whole Crown organ of empire, or Second Sun work of shared survival.

This overlap should be normal. Institutions become sharply exclusive when law, inheritance, sovereignty, or technology is contested.

## 15. Undeveloped powers

The following are required before the political world is complete:

- Named present-day kingdoms and successor blocs.
- Great Houses, rulers, and succession conflicts.
- The government and neutrality of Lasthold.
- Current First Custodian and Crown Regent.
- The leadership structure of the Grand Passage.
- Major Wrightsguild factions and foundry alliances.
- The political settlement ending the War of the Open Forges.
- Countries where Communion, Whole Crown, Banner Court, or Second Sun dominate law.
- The size and territorial reach of each military Order.
- Which factions initially know the truth about Riderless and the Cradle.

## 16. Institutional consistency rules

- Do not map one religion to one country or one Order without explicit development.
- Do not make the Communion purely benevolent or the Whole Crown purely irrational.
- Do not make the Broken Choir uniformly apocalyptic; its services and internal tendencies are part of its danger.
- Do not equate moderate Mortalists with Ash Levelers.
- Do not make every Banner Court follower reject Communion sacraments.
- Do not make Order stewardship an absolute equipment lock.
- Do not use religious terms as decoration without legal, social, or technical consequences.
- Do not let an institution's stated doctrine erase its material interests and contradictions.
