# Characters and Relationships

*Authoritative character inventory and relationship framework. Consolidated baseline: 2026-07-23.*

## 1. Scope and status

This document owns:

- Character identities and roles.
- Allegiances and personal beliefs.
- Relationships and shared history.
- Character arcs and campaign continuity.
- The distinction between playable, supporting, rival, and antagonist roles.

The existing named roster is mostly **Draft**. Several characters are already implemented as combat opponents, but implementation does not automatically freeze biography or narrative arc.

The protagonist and most relationships are **Undeveloped**.

## 2. Protagonist

### Confirmed

- The player becomes or begins play as an armiger.
- The protagonist participates in the Grand Passage.
- The protagonist's father fought in the War of the Open Forges.
- The player eventually confronts the First Armiger before reaching the Cradle.

### Draft

- The protagonist may become central to the conflict over Rebirth and the Final Claimant.
- The protagonist may carry an identity, component, lineage, or authentication state relevant to Rebirth.
- The protagonist may be interpreted differently by the Communion, Whole Crown, Second Sun, Broken Choir, and Ash Levelers.
- Riderless may function as the protagonist's machine mirror.

### Undeveloped

- Name, age, gender presentation, and appearance.
- Home country, class, faith, and starting institution.
- Whether the protagonist begins as squire, commoner, noble, guild pilot, mercenary, or established armiger.
- Personal reason for entering the Grand Passage.
- Relationship with the father.
- Relationship with the First Armiger.
- Starting harness and how it was acquired.
- Knowledge of the Final Edict and Rebirth at the beginning.
- Personality, voice, and degree of player-defined dialogue.
- Character flaw and emotional arc.

Do not lock campaign structure around an assumed chosen-one origin until these decisions are approved.

## 6. Supporting figures

### Herald of the Lists

- Voice of each Passage.
- Oversees the Casting.
- Announces fights and laurels.
- Remembers the armigers who fought.
- Provides a diegetic interface for tournament structure.

**Undeveloped:** name, office structure, political independence, personal agenda, and whether the title belongs to one person or a succession.

### Wrightmother Sella

- Mistress of the Wrightsguild.
- Builds dependable modern equipment.
- Repairs relics she may never be allowed to own.
- Intended shop and upgrade authority.
- Voices the class wound at the setting's center.

**Undeveloped:** history, relationship to Revival foundries, political faction within the guild, and connection to the protagonist.

### Riderless

- Intact imperial executor associated with Rebirth.
- Fights without an acknowledged pilot.
- Waits in the Cradle.
- Denied by Orders and interpreted by several religions.

Riderless is both character and mystery. Do not assign ordinary dialogue, motive, gender, or personhood until the campaign decides what evidence the player encounters.

## 8. Required relationship architecture

Before the campaign is locked, build:

1. A protagonist relationship web with at least one tie to Lasthold, one to the Passage, one to the War of the Open Forges, and one to the final mystery.
2. A rival ladder in which repeated meetings change relationships.
3. At least one character who represents each major ideological answer without becoming a spokesperson only.
4. Personal relationships that cross faction lines.
5. A reason defeated rivals remain relevant after their duel.
6. A clear emotional role for the First Armiger.
7. A human cost attached to Edictbound use before the endgame.
8. Civilian and non-armiger characters who make the world's material stakes visible.

## 9. Character design standard

Every major character should define:

- Public role.
- Private need.
- Material dependence.
- Faction and belief.
- Relationship to harness power.
- Combat doctrine, if an armiger.
- One readable strength.
- One human blind spot.
- What they want from the protagonist.
- What changes after the protagonist defeats, helps, or refuses them.

Characters should not exist only to explain lore. Their institutional position must create a personal pressure, and their personal choices must reveal the institution's contradictions.

## 10. Continuity rules

- Do not equate combat implementation with a finalized character arc.
- Do not make every rival an enemy outside the Lists.
- Do not reveal Vesk's motive only to fill an empty biography.
- Do not decide which Isabeau harness is real without an approved story purpose.
- Do not assume the First Armiger is the protagonist's father.
- Do not define Riderless as person or machine-monster prematurely.
- Do not turn a character's religious affiliation into their entire personality.
- Do not create geography-specific origins until Section 04 has an approved atlas.
