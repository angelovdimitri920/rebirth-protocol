# Campaign and Narrative

*Authoritative campaign framework. Consolidated baseline: 2026-07-23.*

## 1. Scope and status

This document owns:

- Campaign structure and sequence.
- Story revelations and mystery pacing.
- Main quests, side stories, and faction involvement.
- Narrative consequences of gameplay progression.
- Endings and final choices.
- Dialogue and story-delivery principles.

Only the four-part campaign spine and JRPG-style shell are **Confirmed canon**. The detailed campaign remains **Undeveloped**. The scaffold in Sections 5 through 10 is explicitly **Draft** and exists to organize later decisions, not to canonize scenes.

## 2. Locked campaign spine

> **Circuit → Broken Choir → First Armiger → Cradle**

This sequence means:

1. The player enters the public tournament world.
2. The Broken Choir turns tournament advancement into a struggle over forbidden technology and Rebirth.
3. The First Armiger becomes the personal and martial threshold before the truth.
4. The Cradle brings the player into direct contact with Riderless and the Rebirth Protocol.

Do not replace or reorder this spine without explicit approval.

## 3. Campaign promise

An initially ceremonial journey through the Grand Passage gradually exposes the political and existential reason the Passage exists.

The campaign must connect:

- Public competition.
- Personal rivalry.
- Political change after the War of the Open Forges.
- Religious conflict over consent and sovereignty.
- The temptation and cost of Edictbound capability.
- The protagonist's still-undeveloped family and possible mentorship ties.
- The mystery of Riderless and Rebirth.

The final conflict cannot be only "destroy the superweapon." It must ask whether civilization should reconnect a system that might end war by ending refusal.

## 4. Campaign shell

The game is not a menu-only sequence of duels.

The intended shell includes:

- A walkable Lasthold hub.
- Named characters who persist between events.
- Dialogue, shops, workshops, shrines, and faction presence.
- A hangar and test yard.
- Event selection through the Herald's hall.
- Passage events as contained roguelite battle sequences.
- Laurels, spoils, relationships, and story developments returning to the persistent campaign.

A typical story session is:

> **Arrive or return to Lasthold → talk and investigate → shop, fit, and test → enter an event → complete its Passage → receive laurels, spoils, and a story beat → return to a changed hub**

## 5. Draft phase scaffold

### Phase I: Circuit

Purpose:

- Establish Lasthold and the Grand Passage.
- Introduce the protagonist's starting status.
- Teach the Orders, guilds, faiths, and tournament law through people rather than exposition.
- Build recurring relationships with the five standard rivals.
- Show the political effects of Revival-pattern harnesses.
- Establish the father and First Armiger as personal history without explaining everything.

The phase should end when tournament success makes the protagonist impossible for major powers to ignore.

### Phase II: Broken Choir

Purpose:

- Reveal that illegal parts are linked to the Final Edict rather than merely stronger contraband.
- Introduce several Choir tendencies, including sympathetic recruitment and genuinely apocalyptic actors.
- Connect tournament sites, hidden caches, Veiled Restoration research, and Cinderbound raids.
- Force the protagonist to make useful but dangerous choices around Edictbound equipment.
- Deepen the Final Claimant question without confirming the protagonist's nature.

The existing **Broken Choir's Gauntlets** provide a gameplay framework but not a complete story.

### Phase III: First Armiger

Purpose:

- Turn the mystery into a personal confrontation.
- Reveal why the First Armiger accepted the Martyr and Burning Saltire.
- Test whether the protagonist has learned more than combat power.
- Resolve or transform whatever relationship to the First Armiger is approved in Section 06.
- Establish the final path to the Cradle.

If kinship or mentorship is approved, it must be established throughout the campaign rather than introduced only as a surprise twist.

### Phase IV: Cradle

Purpose:

- Bring the protagonist, factions, and accumulated choices into contact with Riderless.
- Present evidence about Rebirth without necessarily answering every metaphysical question.
- Make refusal, restoration, control, destruction, or shared stewardship materially distinct.
- Resolve the protagonist's personal arc.
- Determine the future of the Grand Passage and the harness order.

Whether Riderless is the final boss, ally, judge, victim, or changing combination remains **Draft**.

## 6. Faction stakes in the campaign

| Faction | What it may want from the protagonist or Rebirth |
|---|---|
| Universal Communion | Protect the protagonist while preventing any person from holding unrefusable power |
| Whole Crown | Authenticate, control, imprison, study, or transform the protagonist into a claimant |
| Second Sun / Mortalists | Make power answerable to ordinary communities and break hereditary monopoly |
| Banner Court | Determine lineage, obligations, and whether remembered persons retain a claim |
| Broken Choir | Reveal or create the Final Claimant and complete Rebirth |
| Ash Levelers | Kill the claimant, destroy the harness order, or provoke its collapse |
| Great Houses and Orders | Preserve legitimacy, survive Revival disruption, and control the Passage |
| Wrightsguild | Gain ownership, recognition, and access equal to the technology it sustains |

These are pressures, not predetermined alliances.

## 7. Mystery structure

The campaign should reveal evidence in layers:

1. **Public history:** Final Edict, Severance, Concord, and common religious interpretations.
2. **Institutional secrets:** Hidden archives, Veiled Restoration laboratories, Choir caches, suppressed harness lineages.
3. **Personal evidence:** The father, First Armiger, protagonist's identity, and Riderless Recognition.
4. **Direct encounter:** Rebirth behavior and Riderless actions that challenge all existing interpretations.
5. **Choice under uncertainty:** The player acts without receiving an omniscient answer.

The story may clarify events while preserving metaphysical uncertainty. It should distinguish "the player lacks information" from "the setting deliberately has no confirmed answer."

## 8. Narrative use of Edictbound equipment

Edictbound power should create:

- Immediate tactical temptation.
- Public dishonor through laurel loss.
- Legal and religious consequences.
- Visible contamination.
- Personal memory and identity stakes.
- Different reactions from each faction.

The current build represents the cost through drawbacks and reduced laurels. A future campaign pass must decide whether repeated use affects:

- Dialogue.
- Memory.
- relationships.
- Available endings.
- Harness behavior.
- Hub access.
- Faction trust.
- Permanent contamination.

Do not implement a permanent corruption meter until its narrative consequences are approved.

## 9. Endings

**Undeveloped:** The game has not committed to one ending or multiple endings.

The setting supports several ideological outcomes:

- Permanent Severance or destruction.
- Whole Crown restoration.
- Second Sun public stewardship.
- A negotiated or distributed command structure.
- Completion of the Broken Choir's Rebirth.
- Recognition of Riderless as a person capable of refusal.
- Refusal of every institutional claim.

These are thematic possibilities, not approved ending routes. Any ending must address:

- The protagonist's identity and relationships.
- Riderless.
- The Final Edict.
- The political future of Revival manufacture.
- The Grand Passage.
- The rights of ordinary people living under harness power.

## 10. Side-story framework

Side content should strengthen the main question through local stakes:

- Harness inheritance disputes.
- Adoption of captured components.
- Litany Sister inspections.
- Foundry and guild labor conflicts.
- Second Sun heat allocation.
- Whole Crown archaeology and claimant trials.
- Choir medicine and recruitment.
- Ash Leveler terrorism and state retaliation.
- Local shrines with competing interpretations.
- Echoes of dead pilots and the Law of Quiet Ancestors.
- Rival stories that continue after tournament defeat.

Side quests should not merely distribute lore. Each should ask the player to decide who bears cost, risk, ownership, memory, or authority.

## 11. Story-delivery principles

- Explain institutions through conflict, ritual, work, and personal consequence.
- Let characters disagree with their own faction.
- Use the hub to show civilian life and material needs.
- Make tournament spectacle enjoyable before revealing its political purpose.
- Keep humor and warmth in the circuit so the campaign has something worth saving.
- Avoid lore dumps that settle intentional mysteries.
- Reuse places and characters so relationships accumulate.
- Let defeated rivals change role rather than disappear.
- Make equipment history visible through names, repairs, and bearer lineages.
- Keep every major revelation connected to a present choice.

## 12. Campaign dependencies

The campaign cannot be finalized until:

1. Section 04 establishes Lasthold, the atlas, and major present-day powers.
2. Section 05 defines the governments and leaders acting through each institution.
3. Section 06 defines the protagonist, father, First Armiger, and core relationship web.
4. Section 08 resolves permanent versus run-scoped progression.
5. The project decides whether endings branch.
6. The headless cockpit decision establishes how the protagonist is visually framed inside the harness.

## 13. Required campaign decisions

- Protagonist origin and motivation.
- Inciting incident.
- Father's identity, allegiance, and fate.
- Exact relationship to the First Armiger.
- Why the Grand Passage leads toward the Cradle.
- What Otho knows and why.
- How the Broken Choir identifies the protagonist.
- Which factions know Riderless exists.
- How many locations the campaign visits outside Lasthold.
- Whether the player may equip Edictbound parts without using them.
- Whether contamination is reversible.
- Whether major rivals can die, defect, or become partners.
- One ending versus multiple endings.
- What remains changed in the world after the final choice.

## 14. Narrative consistency rules

- Do not confirm that the protagonist is the Final Claimant before the campaign earns that revelation.
- Do not reduce the Choir to random destruction.
- Do not make the First Armiger's Edictbound use an unexplained boss upgrade.
- Do not reveal Riderless's personhood through exposition alone.
- Do not let a final choice ignore ordinary communities or the political order.
- Do not use multiple endings as a substitute for choosing a coherent protagonist arc.
- Do not finalize acts before the atlas and relationships exist.
